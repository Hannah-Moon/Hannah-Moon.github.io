Revitalizing a Rust Belt City by connecting every level of people in West Pennsylvania

  4. Client/Sponsor: Forward Cities

About Product

KenNext is a WebApp that connects underserved and under-employed populations in Alle-Kiski Valley with resources and employment opportunities through an engaging experience.

Previous Next

KenNext App Screens



Problem and Need

People living in New Kensington are having difficulty accessing information about social service and workforce development opportunities in Alle-Kiski Valley, West Pennsylvania. The communication gap in the city is severe amongst the poor and unemployed. They lack trust in the local ruling body and non-profit organizations and capacity to receive help. Training and job opportunities are often difficult to know due to diffused information channels. The goal is to create a solution for people to easily access training and jobs to move the career ladder upwards.

Insights (Affinity Diagram)

The research data were synthesized using affinity mapping to derive insights, identify target users and product opportunity gaps.
a. People who need social services are recommended by families or social workers before transitioning to workforce training.
b. Existing services information is diffuse.

Persona: Build Empathy with Users

Based on all the research, the team created a precise and narrow scope of solution: creating interactive opportunities for single moms by offering interactive job search, offering mentorship, connecting social services, and finding educational opportunities.



Key User Journey: A life of Single Mom

The team synthesized all our data to draw a “User Journey Map” to clearly visualize the area for the MVP(Minimum Viable Product) opportunity.



Value Proposition Analysis

We examined the value that would be created in the market and for the users by pursuing this opportunity. We used the Value Opportunity Analysis framework which helps us to understand the usable, useful, and desirable aspects of any product/service.
There are 7 classes of value opportunities that are discussed to determine the product/service. They are - emotion, aesthetics, identity, ergonomics, impact, core technology, and quality. Each of these is expanded in a total of 23 attributes which are defined and discussed.
The team defined each value from the perspective of the primary user group (low-income group) and focused on their goals, responsibilities to balance work-life, technology access, digital literacy, and habits.



Product Requirement

After the VOA, we transformed the significant value opportunities into product requirements, and selected the 4 most important requirements as below:
Once we justified our product requirement, we made a summary of our work to confirm the Product Development Opportunity as below:

  • Let key stakeholders build a self-curated network with social service supporting networks to reach financial independence.
  • Support discussions about resources with peers.
  • Design learning tools beyond text resources.

Reflection

Consideration

Because the web app stores personal data, we have a responsibility to protect user data and ensure that the data is being used ethically. This was a key concern from social benefit organizations because it is very important in forming trust between the supporting organizations and key stakeholders.

Possibilities

As the population of single parents is increasing without no suitable education tools, KenNext is a great solution to fill this market gap. The product also can be expanded to other persona groups, such as chronic homeless, additics, and homebound parents.

Challenges & Takeaways

This remains to be one of the most impactful projects that I have worked on so far in my UX career. I wish we could enhance the mentorship function, but because of the trust issues within the community, we decided to wait until they trust this new platform.

What is the JAM?

The JAM case engages teams of data scientists, developers, and designers in a simulation Applied AI project. In 40 minutes, students need to brainstorm about the problem, do research, define a solution, and present an optimal suggestion. The solution should include data analysis, strong design thinking, and engineering architecture. Each team has 3 minutes to present.


About Deloitte's Applied AI JAM Competition







Understand

Our team began to define the rooted causes of the problem before we move onto the research process. Then, the team analyzed the data supplied by Deloitte and dissected the problem: in our COVID-driven virtual world, how can universities increase student viewership of college football games? We spent some time reframing the question and found ourselves asking how might we increase viewership by optimizing interactive experiences. The judges from Deloitte really liked how we reframed the problem statement because we embodied a human-centered design methodology to frame the problem.







Persona

By reframing the opportunity, our team created a precise and narrow scope for our solution. The team focused on creating interactive opportunities, such as offer interactive game experience at home which users can enjoy more playful experience at-home to engage with games on stadiums. We defined key personas. We divided viewrs into two; Frequent Viewers and Non-Frequent Viewers.










Data Analysis

The team decided to use regression model for data analysis because it can be designed to identify both of two personas we defined. We envisioned to find a correlation between different factors that can drive the regression output. We first developed a boxplot to have information on the variability or dispersion of the data. It gave us a good indication of how the values in the data are spread out.




Solution

The data analysis inspired us to create a real-time winner prediction game. Before games begin, the viewer can predict the game scores and main contributors to win the games. The game can be only played when it is on air in order to boost live viewership. If viewers can predict the game based on the live show with analytic report they can refer to predic a winner, it can be more fun than just watching games. By applying intuitive and clean UI/UX, we designed to make the UI interface easy for viewers who are new to the game prediction game.
